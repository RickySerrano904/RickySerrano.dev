This was a custom PC build I put together for my sister, designed for productivity and photo editing. It started as my brother-in-law's Christmas gift idea for her, and he funded the build with a target budget of around $2,000.

## Build Goals

- Create a responsive workstation for photo editing, general productivity, and long-term use.
- Keep the total build around the $2,000 target while still choosing parts that would age well.
- Avoid paying prebuilt workstation pricing for similar performance.
- Make the system look intentional in the room instead of purely utilitarian.

## Hardware and Aesthetic Decisions

- **CPU:** Chosen for strong long-term photo editing and productivity performance. Was a MAJOR upgrade from her previous system.
- **CPU cooler:** AIO liquid cooling was selected to keep the system quiet under load while also fitting the white aesthetic and providing subtle lighting.
- **Motherboard:** A solid, reliable option with good features and future upgrade paths, while avoiding gaming-focused aesthetics.
- **Memory:** 64 GB of fast DDR5 RAM was chosen to ensure smooth performance with large photo libraries and multitasking, even as software demands increase over time.
- **Storage:** A 2 TB NVMe drive was selected to provide fast access to large photo libraries and editing projects. 
- **GPU:** I donated an RTX 2070 Super from one of my older builds to keep the budget practical without weakening the overall experience. Because she isn't gaming on this pc, this was a practical choice.
- **PSU:** A reliable, fully modular power supply with more than enough wattage to support the current build.
- **Fans:** Added extra case fans selected for a balance of cooling performance, airflow, and low noise.
- **Case:** White Fractal North, chosen because its white panels and wood-grain front intake fit naturally with her home's bohemian aesthetic.
- **Cable extensions:** White sleeved extensions added so the interior matched the case and felt more finished.
- **Budget:** Being able to reuse a GPU helped stay around the $2,000 target while still leaving room for strong core components.

## What I Learned

This build was a good reminder that a PC project is not only about raw specs. The final system needed to fit the user, the room, the budget, and the intended workload. It may be more machine than she strictly needs right now, but it should serve her well for years and still came in as a better value than many prebuilt photo editing PCs with similar advertised specs.

I was honored to build it for them, and I had a lot of fun putting together something that felt both personal and practical.
